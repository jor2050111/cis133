CIS133 Skills Lab 1A: Source Profiles
Five web sources the computer club found while researching a
resource page about recycling old electronics. All five profiles are
fictional composites written for this course. Evaluate them exactly
as described. They do not contain live URLs.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
PROFILE A
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Address type: Official local government site (.gov)
Site:         City of Desert Valley, Public Works Department
Author:       No individual byline. Published by the department.
Last updated: March 2026
Purpose:      Informs residents how and where to recycle electronics.
Content:      Lists the city's four drop-off locations with hours,
              names which devices are accepted, and links to the city
              ordinance that bans electronics from curbside bins.
Other notes:  Plain design, no ads. A "Contact Public Works" email
              appears in the footer.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
PROFILE B
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Address type: Personal blog (.blog)
Site:         Gadget Guru, a personal tech blog
Author:       "GadgetGuru88" (no real name or bio provided)
Last updated: August 2019
Purpose:      Argues that consumer recycling programs waste effort.
Content:      A long opinion post claiming most collected electronics
              end up in landfills anyway. Mixes a few statistics
              (without saying where they came from) with personal
              stories and complaints.
Other notes:  Comment section is full of arguments. No sources cited
              anywhere in the post.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
PROFILE C
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Address type: Local news outlet (.com)
Site:         Desert Valley Times, a local daily newspaper
Author:       Maria Contreras, staff reporter (byline links to her
              other articles)
Last updated: November 2025
Purpose:      Reports on a rise in electronic waste after the holiday
              season.
Content:      Quotes the city's recycling coordinator and a state
              environmental report, gives collection totals for the
              past three years, and lists upcoming collection events.
Other notes:  The site shows the first three paragraphs free, then
              asks readers to subscribe.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
PROFILE D
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Address type: Commercial advice site (.info)
Site:         Best Tech Tips
Author:       "Editorial Team" (no individuals named)
Last updated: No date anywhere on the page
Purpose:      Unclear. The page mixes general advice with product
              links and heavy advertising.
Content:      Long, repetitive sections that restate the same generic
              tips ("recycling is good for the planet") in slightly
              different words. No local information, no named
              programs, no citations.
Other notes:  A banner offers "100+ free recycling images, no
              attribution needed!" but the page shows no license text
              and does not say who created the images.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
PROFILE E
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Address type: College site (.edu)
Site:         Canyon State College, Office of Sustainability
Author:       Dr. Lena Ortiz, sustainability coordinator (named on
              the page with contact information)
Last updated: September 2024
Purpose:      Educates students on e-waste and promotes the campus
              collection program.
Content:      Explains what counts as e-waste, cites two state
              environmental reports with links, and posts the campus
              collection-event calendar for the academic year.
Other notes:  Part of the college's official site. The event calendar
              stops at the end of the 2024-2025 academic year.
