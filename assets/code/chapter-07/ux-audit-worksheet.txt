Use one copy of this worksheet per site. Work through the five
principles in order. For each one, follow the "what to check"
prompt, then record at least one strength and one gap where they
exist. A strength names something the site does well and where you
saw it. A gap names something the site fails to do and where a
visitor would feel it. Point at pages and regions, not feelings:
"the contact page's schedule" beats "it seems fine."

---------------------------------------------------------------------
1. USABILITY
---------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition: visitors can complete their tasks without instructions
or guesswork.

What to check: pick the site's most likely visitor task and try to
complete it yourself, counting every stop where you had to guess
what to do next.

2. ACCESSIBILITY
---------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition: the site works for every visitor, including people
using screen readers, keyboards, or other assistive tools.

What to check: confirm every image has fitting alt text, then put
the mouse away and Tab through each page, watching whether focus
stays visible at every stop.

3. CONSISTENCY
---------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition: the same things look and behave the same way on every
page, so nothing has to be relearned.

What to check: visit every page and compare the header, the
navigation, the colors, and the type. Note anything that changes
between pages, and anything a visitor would expect to find but
cannot.
