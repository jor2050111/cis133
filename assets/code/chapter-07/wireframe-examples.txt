=====================================================================
CIS133 WIREFRAME EXAMPLES: COPY AND ADAPT
=====================================================================

Two skeleton wireframes of a generic page, one at desktop width and
one at phone width. Copy either frame into your own plan and adapt
it: rename the content boxes, add or remove boxes, and resize
regions to fit the page you are planning. Keep the landmark labels
(header, nav, main, footer) on whatever boxes play those roles.

Wireframes are deliberately grayscale. Boxes and labels only. No
colors, no fonts. Those decisions belong to the stylesheet, later.

You may sketch on paper instead and photograph the result. Both
forms are accepted wherever a wireframe is required. Draw text
frames with plain keyboard characters (+, -, |) so they survive
any viewer.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
READING THE FRAMES
---------------------------------------------------------------------

* Every outer box is a landmark you already build in HTML: header,
  nav, main, footer.
* Boxes that sit side by side are a flex line. Boxes that stack are
  the normal flow, no layout rule needed.
* The phone frame stacks what the desktop frame lays in rows. Note
  which rows wrap and which regions simply narrow.
